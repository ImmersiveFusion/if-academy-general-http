# What this publication refuses to do

*An editorial charter, by Immersive Fusion. Dated 2026-05-25. Reviewed annually; the date below advances when the charter is re-affirmed; a year in which the date does not advance is a visible governance signal that the charter has not been actively endorsed in the prior period.*

This publication is not a sales channel for Immersive Fusion. It is not a recruiting channel. It is not a curriculum or a school program. It is not a place where ideas are softened to make them easier to sell, or sharpened to make them easier to share. It is a place where we write down what we have learned about AI in a voice that does not flatter the reader and does not fear them.

We are an observability company. We watch software systems for a living. That work has taught us specific things about how AI assistants behave when no one is watching, and we think those things are useful to people outside our trade. We are writing them down here, in language a person who does not work in software can read.

To make that possible, we refuse the following:

**We refuse to sell on these pages.** No piece on this site links to our pricing page, our demo form, our signup flow, or any page whose primary purpose is to make you a customer. The byline at the bottom of every piece says *"by Immersive Fusion."* That is the only commercial signal on the page, and it is there because hiding who wrote a thing is worse than naming who wrote it.

**We refuse to capture leads.** This site does not collect your email. It does not have a newsletter that pipes you into our sales process. It does not track you for retargeting. The privacy notice tells you what is logged by the servers that run the site, because some logging is unavoidable, and we would rather tell you than not.

**We refuse to publish on a schedule.** Nothing here comes out because the calendar says it is time. Pieces come out when there is something true enough to say. If a year passes between publications, that is the publication being honest about what it knows. We do not publish to stay relevant.

**We refuse to write in the two voices that dominate writing about AI.** We will not tell you the world is ending. We will not tell you the world is about to be saved. Both are easier to write than what we are trying to write, and both fail the reader. When we slip toward one of them, the piece does not ship.

**We refuse to pretend AI is going away.** This is not an AI-skepticism site. The premise we work from is that AI is here, that you will use it whether or not we publish, and that the useful work is helping you use it in a way that does not damage you or anyone else. If you came here looking for permission to ignore AI, you will not find it.

**We refuse to be a recruiting funnel.** We are not trying to interest your kid in working in software. We are not trying to interest you in working at Immersive Fusion. If something we publish makes someone want to do either, that is their choice and we are glad for them, but it is not what we are publishing for, and it is not how we will measure whether we are succeeding.

**We refuse to enter classroom partnerships that require us to soften the work.** Every booklet and essay we publish is licensed Creative Commons BY-NC 4.0, which means a teacher can print and use any of it in any classroom without asking us, without paying us, and without our knowing. (Our underlying reference materials — the AI tenets we describe, the link policy that governs us, the patterns we extract from our own writing — are licensed Creative Commons BY 4.0 so that anyone can cite, build on, or align their own AI work with them.) We are not building a curriculum. We are not partnering with a curriculum project that would require us to change what we publish to fit their sequence. The materials are here to be used; they are not here to be co-branded.

**We refuse to write in fear of being criticized.** We will get things wrong. When we do, we will say so on the same site, dated, and we will not delete the original. The publication exists because there are real questions about AI that the loudest voices on either side are not answering well. Some of our answers will turn out to be wrong too. We would rather be wrong on the record than safe in private.

**We refuse to be soft about a hard thing.** The voice of this publication is calm. The voice is not cautious. Calm is what we owe the reader; cautious would be what we owed ourselves. We will name things by their names. We will not euphemize because the topic is uncomfortable. We will translate, when translation helps the reader; we will not pretend a serious idea is simpler than it is.

If any of these refusals is ever broken — a paid product placement, a lead-capture form, a piece killed because it would not have driven a sale — that breach will be visible to anyone who reads carefully. We have built the publication so that the breach would show. If you ever notice one, write to us at the address in the footer of `immersivefusion.com`. We will answer.

*— by Immersive Fusion, 2026-05-25*
