# Our editorial commitments

*A charter, by Immersive Fusion. Dated 2026-05-25.*

*Reviewed annually. If the date above doesn't advance in a calendar year, that's a signal: the commitments here haven't been actively endorsed in the prior period.*

This is the full version of [the About page](about.md). It's longer because the substance matters, and a careful reader should be able to hold us to specifics.

## We are an observability company

We watch software systems for a living. That work has taught us specific things about how AI assistants behave when no one is watching, and we think those things are useful to people outside our trade. We are writing them down here, in language a person who does not work in software can read.

## No selling on these pages

No piece on this site links to our pricing page, our demo form, our signup flow, or any page whose primary purpose is to make you a customer. The byline at the bottom of every piece says *"by Immersive Fusion."* That is the only commercial signal on the page, and it is there because hiding who wrote a thing is worse than naming who wrote it.

## No lead capture

This site does not collect your email. It does not have a newsletter that pipes you into our sales process. It does not track you for retargeting. The privacy notice tells you what is logged by the servers that run the site, because some logging is unavoidable, and we would rather tell you than not.

## No publication schedule

Nothing here comes out because the calendar says it is time. Pieces come out when there is something true enough to say. If a year passes between publications, that is the publication being honest about what it knows. We do not publish to stay relevant.

## Not the two voices that dominate writing about AI

We will not tell you the world is ending. We will not tell you the world is about to be saved. Both are easier to write than what we are trying to write, and both fail the reader. When we slip toward one of them, the piece does not ship.

## AI isn't going away

This is not an AI-skepticism site. The premise we work from is that AI is here, that you will use it whether or not we publish, and that the useful work is helping you use it in a way that does not damage you or anyone else. If you came here looking for permission to ignore AI, you will not find it.

## Not a recruiting funnel

We are not trying to interest your kid in working in software. We are not trying to interest you in working at Immersive Fusion. If something we publish makes someone want to do either, that is their choice and we are glad for them, but it is not what we are publishing for, and it is not how we will measure whether we are succeeding.

The audience we write for is anyone who will live alongside AI: the future doctor, teacher, plumber, artist, parent. The counselor. The neighbor. They will have better intuitions because they read this. That's the goal.

## No classroom partnerships that soften the work

Every booklet and essay we publish is licensed [Creative Commons BY-NC 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/4.0/), so any teacher, parent, or school can print and use any of it for free, without asking us, without paying us, without our knowing. (Our underlying reference materials, the AI tenets we describe, the link policy that governs us, the patterns we extract from our own writing, are licensed [Creative Commons BY 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/) so that anyone can cite, build on, or align their own AI work with them.)

We are not building a curriculum. We are not partnering with a curriculum project that would require us to change what we publish to fit their sequence. The materials are here to be used. They are not here to be co-branded.

## We will get things wrong

When we do, we'll say so on the same site, dated, and we will not delete the original. Some of our answers will turn out to be wrong too. We would rather be wrong on the record than safe in private.

## Calm without being cautious

The voice of this publication is calm. The voice is not cautious. Calm is what we owe the reader; cautious would be what we owe ourselves. We name things by their names. We don't euphemize because the topic is uncomfortable. We translate when translation helps the reader; we don't pretend a serious idea is simpler than it is.

## If you notice something off

If any of these commitments is ever broken, a paid product placement, a lead-capture form, a piece killed because it would not have driven a sale, that breach will be visible to anyone who reads carefully. We have built the publication so that the breach would show. If you ever notice one, write to us at [academy@immersivefusion.com](mailto:academy@immersivefusion.com). We will answer.

*— by Immersive Fusion, 2026-05-25*
